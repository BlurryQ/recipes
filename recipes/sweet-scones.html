Sweet Scones

Description

Mary Berry's scones are the perfect easy tea-time treat, whether jam-and-cream-topped (in whichever order you like), plain or packed with sultanas.

Equipment

  • Baking tray
  • Mixing bowl
  • Rolling pin
  • 5cm cutter

Ingredients

  • 450g self-raising flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 50g caster sugar
  • 100g butter (softened)
  • 50ml milk
  • 2 eggs
  • sultanas (optional)

Method

  1. Step 1

    2. Preheat the oven to 220C/200C Fan/Gas 7. Lightly grease two baking trays.

  2. Step 2

    3. Put the flour, baking powder and sugar in a large bowl. Add the butter and rub in with your fingertips until the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs.

  3. Step 3

    4. Crack the eggs into a measuring jug, then add enough milk to make the total liquid 300ml/10fl oz. Stir the egg and milk into the flour – you may not need it all – and mix to a soft, sticky dough.

  4. Step 4

    5. Turn out onto a lightly floured work surface, knead lightly and work in the sultanas, if using. Roll out to a rectangle about 2cm/¾in thick.

  5. Step 5

    6. Cut into as many rounds as possible with a fluted 5cm/2in cutter and place them on the prepared baking trays. Brush the tops of the scones with a little extra milk, or any egg and milk left in the jug.

  6. Step 6

    7. Bake for 12–15 minutes, or until the scones are well risen and a pale, golden-brown colour. Lift onto a wire rack to cool. Eat as fresh as possible.
